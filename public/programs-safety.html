A simple safety planning flow

1

Assess risks and routines

Identify potential wandering triggers, evaluate home and community environments, and map out daily routines to understand where safety measures are most needed.

2

Set up identification and communication

Establish ID systems like medical alert bracelets, communication cards, and emergency contact protocols to ensure your child can be quickly identified and assisted if separated.

3

Coordinate with school and community

Share your safety plan with educators, neighbors, and local first responders so everyone understands autism safety resources and wandering prevention strategies for your child.

4

Practice and update the plan

Regularly rehearse safety procedures with your child, review and adjust strategies as they grow, and keep contact information current to maintain effective protection.

Everyday safety guidance

Practical steps to help create safer environments for individuals with autism in daily life.

Home readiness

Secure windows and doors with appropriate locks and alarms. Consider visual schedules and clear boundaries to help establish safe routines.

Create a calm, predictable environment that reduces anxiety and supports independence while maintaining necessary safety measures.

School coordination

Share safety profiles with teachers and staff, including communication preferences and potential triggers. Establish clear protocols for transitions and emergencies.

Regular communication between home and school ensures consistency in safety approaches and quick response when needed.

Community awareness

Help local first responders understand autism-specific safety needs. Consider identification tools and practice community outings in advance.

Building relationships with neighbors and community members creates a supportive network for autism safety resources and wandering prevention.
